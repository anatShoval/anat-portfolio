My final project - A primary diagnosis of dementia

Dementia, is a common ailment among elderly people. While common, many sufferers wait years for a diagnosis, for a variety of reasons. Failing to diagnose the illness might stem from a reluctant patient, family members brushing off symptoms as normal aging behavior or accepting the illness as a given. Our project allows a more efficient and effective use of a diagnostic tool based on Dr. Meiron's cognitive task. As a first step, we visited Dr. Meiron's laboratory at Herzog hospital, to fully understand in person the complexities of administering and analyzing a cognitive task. After researching and analyzing the data, we arrived at two target demographics: clinicians and patients. In addition, we took into account Dr. Meiron's research and created a system administrator so he could continue updating the tasks and collecting data. Our solution was a web-based system, available at every computer with an internet connection. Processes within this system would be simple and short as possible, keeping the learning curve to a minimum. Our research shows that the system we developed is easy to use, intuitive and provides an effective and reliable diagnostic tool to those who need it.
